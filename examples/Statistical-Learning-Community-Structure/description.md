# Community Structure Statistical Learning

Replication of Experiment 2 from Schapiro et al. (2013), *Nature Neuroscience*.

## Task

Participants view a continuous stream of 15 abstract symbols generated by a
random walk on a graph with **community structure**: 15 nodes organised into 3
densely connected clusters of 5 (each node has exactly 4 neighbours).

**Cover task (both phases):** press **F** if the symbol appears in its normal
orientation, **J** if it has been rotated 90°. Approximately 20 % of symbols
are rotated. Participants respond on every trial.

## Phases

| Phase | Trials | Sequence type |
|---|---|---|
| Exposure | 1,400 | Pure random walk |
| Parsing | 600 | Alternating 15-item random-walk and Hamiltonian-path blocks |

**Parsing task:** also press **SPACE** at any moment that feels like a natural
boundary or beginning. No feedback is given; there are no right or wrong answers.

## Graph

Three communities of 5 nodes (C0 = 0–4, C1 = 5–9, C2 = 10–14).
Within each community the graph is K5 minus the edge between the two boundary
nodes. Cross-community edges: 4↔5, 9↔10, 14↔0.

## Hamiltonian path (Experiment 2 procedure)

One fixed Hamiltonian cycle is used for all Hamiltonian blocks. Each block is
entered at the node where the preceding random-walk block ended; traversal
direction (forward/backward) is chosen randomly per block.

## Reference

Schapiro, A. C., Rogers, T. T., Cordova, N. I., Turk-Browne, N. B., &
Botvinick, M. M. (2013). Neural representations of events arise from temporal
community structure. *Nature Neuroscience*, 16(4), 486–492.
https://doi.org/10.1038/nn.3331
